Star Wars Jedi: Survivor

Star Wars Jedi: Survivor, released in 2023, is an action-adventure game developed by Respawn Entertainment and published by Electronic Arts. Serving as the sequel to Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order (2019), the game unfolds five years after the events of its predecessor. The storyline continues the journey of Cal Kestis, a young Jedi Knight, and his companions as they grapple with the challenges of surviving the oppressive rule of the Galactic Empire.

The game, initially launched for PlayStation 5, Windows, and Xbox Series X/S on April 28, 2023, expanded its accessibility with plans for versions on PlayStation 4 and Xbox One currently in development. In Star Wars Jedi: Survivor, players follow Cal Kestis in an epic adventure, navigating through a galaxy rife with danger and intrigue, providing an immersive experience for fans of the Star Wars universe.

Star Wars Jedi Survivor Boss Tier List Best Characters

Star Wars Jedi: Survivor Gameplay 

Star Wars Jedi: Survivor, released in 2023, brings exciting gameplay improvements and new features to the Star Wars gaming universe. Building on its predecessor, Fallen Order, the game maintains familiar lightsaber combat mechanics while introducing five unique lightsaber types for Cal Kestis, each with distinct features.

Star Wars Jedi: Survivor Development 

The development of Star Wars Jedi: Survivor began in January 2022, and the game was officially titled in May 2022 with a CGI teaser trailer unveiled at Star Wars Celebration. Created by Respawn Entertainment, the game was released on April 28, 2023, for PlayStation 5, Windows, and Xbox Series X/S.

Article continues below advertisementArticle continues below advertisement

According to game director Stig Asmussen, the use of real-time ray tracing and next-gen capabilities allowed Respawn to achieve a level of quality surpassing their previous works.

Initially designed exclusively for ninth-generation consoles to take advantage of faster load times, Survivor later expanded to include versions for PlayStation 4 and Xbox One, even though the game's development no longer needed to support these older consoles due to the advanced features of the PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S.
